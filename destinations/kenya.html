🇰🇪 Kenya

The Greatest Wildlife Show on Earth

⭐ 4.8 Rating📅 Jul–Oct (Great Migration), Jan–Feb💰 $150–$500/day

Kenya is the safari — the original, the definitive, the one everything else is measured against. No other destination combines the drama of the Great Migration (1.5 million wildebeest, 200,000 zebra, and 350,000 gazelle moving across the savanna, crossing the Mara River as crocodiles wait below) with permanent resident populations of lion, leopard, cheetah, elephant, and rhino, against the backdrop of open savanna and the vast East African sky.

The Maasai Mara: Kenya's Wildlife Crown Jewel

The Maasai Mara National Reserve — contiguous with Tanzania's Serengeti — holds some of Africa's densest permanent wildlife populations: big cats, elephant herds, enormous buffalo, hippos in the Mara River, and hundreds of bird species including nearly all of Africa's raptors. July through October brings the Great Migration's river crossings, arguably the most dramatic natural spectacle on earth. Wildebeest by the thousand plunge into the crocodile-filled Mara River in frenzied, instinct-driven crossing events that last seconds and leave you trembling.

Amboseli: Elephants Against Kilimanjaro

Amboseli National Park, at the foot of Kilimanjaro on the Tanzanian border, is famous for its large-tusked elephant populations — the bull elephants here are some of the largest on earth. Dawn at Amboseli, when the mountain is clear (it clouds over most mornings by 9am), with elephant silhouettes moving across the swamp against Africa's highest peak, is one of the most profoundly beautiful natural scenes you will ever witness. Invest in a good telephoto lens.

Kenya's Indian Ocean Coast

The Kenyan coast offers a completely different experience — and an ideal post-safari counterpoint. Diani Beach, south of Mombasa, has white sand, warm water, and excellent snorkeling. Lamu, a UNESCO World Heritage Site accessible only by boat or light aircraft, is a remarkably preserved Swahili trading town whose donkeys still outnumber cars and whose Omani-influenced architecture dates to the 14th century. Watamu Marine National Park protects extraordinary coral reefs and sea turtle nesting beaches.

  • Book safari accommodation 6–12 months ahead for July–October migration season
  • A private conservancy often offers better game viewing and fewer vehicles than the main Mara reserve
  • Morning drives (6–9am) and late afternoon drives (4–7pm) are best — animals rest midday
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate required if arriving from certain countries — check requirements
  • Budget travelers: Nairobi National Park (25 minutes from the CBD) offers remarkably good wildlife viewing
  • Tipping your guide well is expected and important — they are highly skilled professionals
