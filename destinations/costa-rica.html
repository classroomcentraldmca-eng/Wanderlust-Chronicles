🇨🇷 Costa Rica

Pura Vida: Biodiversity, Rainforest & Volcanic Beauty

⭐ 4.8 Rating📅 December–April (Pacific dry season)💰 $70–$180/day

Costa Rica is the world's greatest argument for the economic value of biodiversity conservation. This small Central American country — smaller than the state of West Virginia — contains 5% of the world's entire biodiversity within its borders. It generates 99% of its electricity from renewable sources, has largely rebuilt its forest cover from 26% in 1983 to over 52% today, and is consistently ranked among the world's happiest nations. It is also extremely beautiful.

National Parks: Biodiversity on Extraordinary Display

Costa Rica's national parks protect over 25% of the country's land — an extraordinary commitment. Manuel Antonio National Park on the Pacific coast combines some of Costa Rica's most beautiful beaches with dense rainforest where three-toed sloths hang motionless in the canopy, white-faced capuchin monkeys raid tourist bags, and scarlet macaws fly overhead in pairs. Tortuguero, on the Caribbean coast, is a vital nesting site for four endangered sea turtle species; watching a giant leatherback emerge from the surf to lay eggs at midnight is genuinely moving. Corcovado, on the remote Osa Peninsula, is what National Geographic once called "the most biologically intense place on earth" — a last refuge for large wildlife including tapirs, jaguars, and harpy eagles.

Arenal & Monteverde: Volcanoes & Cloud Forest

Arenal Volcano, in northern Costa Rica, anchors the country's most popular tourist region. The volcano is active — lava flows and pyroclastic activity are visible from the town of La Fortuna in clear weather. The geothermal hot springs at the volcano's base are legendary (Tabacón Resort's garden hot springs are among the most beautiful in the world). Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve, perpetually wrapped in mist at 1,400m elevation, is one of the world's most important biological reserves. Golden toads were discovered here (and went extinct here). The resplendent quetzal — one of the world's most beautiful birds — nests in its forests.

The Pura Vida Philosophy

"Pura vida" — pure life — functions in Costa Rica as greeting, farewell, thank-you, you're welcome, and a general philosophical orientation toward life. Costa Ricans use it constantly and genuinely. The country scores extremely high on international happiness indices, with excellent healthcare and high literacy rates despite a modest per-capita GDP. Spend meaningful time with Costa Rican families and the source of the country's contentment becomes apparent: strong community bonds, proximity to extraordinary nature, and a deliberate national decision to invest in human wellbeing over military spending.

  • A 4WD rental vehicle is essential — many secondary roads, especially in rainy season, require it
  • Book eco-lodges in remote parks months ahead — Corcovado lodges have very limited capacity
  • Guided night walks in any rainforest reveal an extraordinary nocturnal world invisible by day
  • Tip naturalist guides generously — Costa Rican guides are world-class naturalists and professionals
  • The dry season is December–April on the Pacific; Caribbean coast is drier May–September
  • Pura vida applies to schedules too — embrace the pace and don't over-schedule your days
