🇲🇻 Maldives

Paradise Found: Crystal Waters & Overwater Villas

⭐ 4.8 Rating📅 November–April (dry season)💰 $300–$1500+/day

The Maldives is what the travel industry means when it uses the word 'paradise' — 1,200 coral islands scattered across the Indian Ocean like turquoise jewels, their lagoons so luminously blue-green that photographs look digitally enhanced. They are not. Stretching across 26 natural atolls, this archipelago nation hosts some of the world's most biodiverse coral reefs and the most instagrammed bungalows on earth.

Choosing Your Island & Resort

In the Maldives, most resort islands are entirely private — you arrive by speedboat or seaplane and have the island largely to yourself. This is the fundamental experience. Research your resort before booking: some are surrounded by shallow lagoons perfect for paddleboarding and non-swimmers; others have deeper house reefs excellent for serious snorkelers; others still are primarily dive destinations. The geography of each atoll matters more than the star rating.

The Underwater World

The real Maldives is 80% ocean, and the real experience is underwater. The coral reefs here are among the most biodiverse on earth — though bleaching events in warm El Niño years have caused damage that ongoing marine conservation efforts are working to reverse. Maafushi Island is famous for snorkeling with whale sharks in season (October–May). Banana Reef in North Malé Atoll is one of the dive world's iconic sites. At night on Vaadhoo Island, the beach glows electric blue from bioluminescent phytoplankton — a sight so extraordinary that travelers often describe it as one of the most beautiful things they have ever seen.

Local Islands: The Other Maldives

Not all Maldives travel requires a luxury resort budget. The local island system allows independent travelers to stay on inhabited Maldivian islands — Maafushi, Thulusdhoo, Dhiffushi — at guesthouses for $60–120/night. You get genuine Maldivian culture, excellent snorkeling, and a completely different perspective on the archipelago. Alcohol is unavailable on local islands (only on resort islands), but the food, the people, and the sea are the same extraordinary quality.

  • Book seaplane transfers at least 2 weeks ahead — they operate only in daylight and sell out
  • Dry season November–April is best; May–October is monsoon but significantly cheaper
  • Pack reef-safe sunscreen — standard chemical sunscreens damage coral reefs
  • Many high-end resorts include meals — check what's included before booking
  • Mosquitoes are active at dawn and dusk year-round — bring effective repellent
  • The snorkeling right off your beach is often as good as any boat excursion
