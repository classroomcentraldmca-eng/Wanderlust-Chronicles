🇮🇹 Italy

La Dolce Vita Awaits

⭐ 4.8 Rating📅 April–June, September–October💰 $100–$250/day🛂 Schengen Visa

Italy is the world's most irresistible country — a place where art, architecture, cuisine, fashion, and natural beauty converge at every turn. From the ruins of ancient Rome to the Renaissance masterpieces of Florence, from the labyrinthine waterways of Venice to the terraced fishing villages of the Ligurian coast, Italy overwhelms the senses with relentless, generous beauty.

Rome: The Eternal City

Rome's greatest achievement is making 3,000 years of history feel vivid and immediate. The Colosseum, where 50,000 spectators once watched gladiatorial combat, still imposes. The Forum Romanum is the ghost of an empire laid bare. The Pantheon — perfectly preserved after 2,000 years, its unreinforced concrete dome still the largest in the world — stops visitors in their tracks. Book all major sites in advance: queues without pre-booking are punishing.

Between monuments, eat. Eat carbonara the way Romans eat it — al dente rigatoni, guanciale, pecorino, egg yolk, black pepper, no cream — at a trattoria with handwritten menus and terracotta walls. Eat supplì (fried rice balls) from a hole-in-the-wall. Eat pizza al taglio by weight. The Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel require a full half-day and pre-booking; Caravaggio's paintings in the church of Santa Maria del Popolo are free and extraordinary.

Florence & Tuscany

Florence is a city-sized art gallery. The Uffizi contains the greatest Renaissance collection on earth — Botticelli's Birth of Venus, Leonardo's Annunciation, Michelangelo's Doni Tondo. The Accademia holds Michelangelo's David. The Duomo's Brunelleschi dome is the architectural achievement of the 15th century. Reserve days for slower pleasures: Chianti wine at a farmhouse in Greve, truffle hunting in San Miniato, the perfectly preserved medieval city of Siena.

Venice: Uniquely, Improbably Beautiful

Venice is built on wooden piles driven into the mud of a lagoon 1,500 years ago, and it is sinking at roughly 2mm per year. Go now, and go in winter or shoulder season. November–February Venice is misty, mysterious, and populated almost entirely by Venetians. The tourist-free campo squares, the Rialto fish market at dawn, the Byzantine mosaics of the Basilica di San Marco — they hit differently when you have them to yourself.

The Amalfi Coast & Southern Italy

The Amalfi Coast — 50km of road carved into vertical limestone cliffs above the Tyrrhenian Sea — is among the world's most dramatic coastal drives. The towns of Positano, Ravello, and Amalfi itself are stacked up the hillsides like tilting wedding cakes. Continue south to Puglia, where baroque cities like Lecce are carved entirely from golden limestone, and the conical trulli houses of Alberobello look like a Brothers Grimm illustration.

  • Book Vatican, Colosseum, and Uffizi weeks in advance — they sell out completely in high season
  • Trenitalia and Italo rail passes are excellent for city-hopping; book Frecciarossa trains early
  • At a café, espresso consumed standing at the bar costs less than seated at a table — it's tradition
  • Avoid restaurants immediately adjacent to major tourist sites — quality drops and prices rise dramatically
  • Aperitivo hour (6–9pm) in Milan and Bologna often includes substantial free snacks with a drink
  • Pack comfortable shoes — cobblestones are beautiful but unforgiving after eight hours
