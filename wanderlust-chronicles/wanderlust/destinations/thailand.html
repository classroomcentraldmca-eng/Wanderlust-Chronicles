🇹🇭 Thailand

Land of Smiles, Temples & Islands

⭐ 4.8 Rating📅 November–April (dry season)💰 $40–$120/day

Thailand has been hosting international travelers for half a century — and it remains one of the world's most rewarding destinations for excellent reason. It delivers an exceptional combination of cultural richness, natural beauty, world-class street food, and astonishing value. The Kingdom stretches from the jungles of the far north to the crystalline islands of the south, with Bangkok — one of the world's great cities — at its center.

Bangkok: Controlled Chaos & Hidden Temples

Bangkok rewards travelers who embrace its contradictions. Soaring glass towers above the Chao Phraya River share skyline with ancient temple spires. Sky trains and river express boats ferry commuters past gilded wats and traditional wooden shophouses. Air-conditioned mega-malls exist within walking distance of canals where vendors still sell noodles from wooden boats, as they have for generations. The Grand Palace and Wat Pho are truly unmissable. So is a sunset cruise on the Chao Phraya, an evening in a rooftop bar, and a dawn visit to Wat Arun, the Temple of Dawn, when the riverside light is extraordinary.

Chiang Mai: Culture, Elephants & Mountains

Chiang Mai, in a broad mountain-ringed valley in northern Thailand, feels a world away from Bangkok. The old city is encircled by a moat and contains over 300 temples. The Night Bazaar and Sunday Walking Street markets are outstanding. The surrounding mountains hide ethical elephant sanctuaries where rescued elephants live in relative freedom; walking beside these animals through forest is one of Thailand's most moving experiences. The Doi Inthanon National Park offers Thailand's highest peak, spectacular royal gardens, and some of the best bird watching in Southeast Asia.

The Southern Islands: Choosing Your Paradise

Thailand's southern islands present a genuine dilemma of choice. Koh Samui is the largest and most developed — good infrastructure, beautiful Chaweng Beach, and full-moon party adjacent. Koh Tao is Southeast Asia's best-value diving destination — open water courses available cheaply, excellent coral reefs. Koh Lanta is quieter and more family-friendly. Krabi and Railay Beach — the latter accessible only by boat, hemmed in by vertical limestone karst — is one of Asia's most dramatic coastal settings.

  • Cover shoulders and knees for temple visits — lightweight sarongs work well
  • The BTS Skytrain and MRT metro are the best ways around Bangkok — cheap and reliable
  • Thailand's street food is excellent and generally very safe — eat where locals eat
  • High season is November–March; Andaman coast (Krabi, Railay) best October–April
  • Negotiate tuk-tuk and songthaew fares before you get in — always agree on price first
  • A Thai cooking class is one of travel's best investments — skills and recipes you will use for life
