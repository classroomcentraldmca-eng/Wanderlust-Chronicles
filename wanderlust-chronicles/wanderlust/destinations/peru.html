🇵🇪 Peru

Inca Mysteries, Amazon Wonder & Andean Beauty

⭐ 4.8 Rating📅 May–October (dry season)💰 $60–$150/day

Peru is one of the world's great civilizational travel destinations, layered with pre-Inca cultures, Inca achievements, and colonial history, set against landscapes of extraordinary drama: the Andes, the Amazon, and the desert coast. At its heart is Machu Picchu — the 15th-century Inca citadel that regularly tops polls of the world's most impressive heritage sites, and still manages to exceed every expectation.

Machu Picchu: Managing the Experience

Machu Picchu operates on a strict timed-entry system with around 4,500 visitors per day across multiple slots. You must book tickets in advance at machupicchu.gob.pe — do this months ahead for June through August. The citadel is divided into circuits; Circuit 1 is the classic upper route with the most iconic viewpoints. Arrive for your earliest possible slot and ascend Huayna Picchu mountain (separate ticket, extremely limited numbers) for the aerial view looking down over the citadel from above. The afternoon light, when most day-trippers have left, is often better for photography — and the crowds thin dramatically after 2pm.

Cusco & the Sacred Valley

Cusco (3,400m elevation) was the capital of the Inca Empire, and its historic center is one of the Americas' most extraordinary urban environments — Spanish colonial churches built directly on Inca stone foundations, the precise polygonal masonry of the Incas visible at street level throughout the city. The Sacred Valley below offers the best-preserved Inca site open for exploration (Pisac), the most intact Inca town in existence (Ollantaytambo), and extraordinary textiles in the markets. Acclimatize here for 48 hours before ascending further.

Lima: South America's Culinary Capital

Lima is consistently dismissed as a transit hub by travelers making straight for Cusco. This is an error. The Peruvian capital has become, over the past 20 years, one of the world's great food cities. Virgilio Martínez's Central restaurant, ranked among the world's top restaurants, explores ecosystems along Peru's altitude gradient from Amazon to high Andes. But even without the fine dining: ceviche at a Miraflores cevichería on a sunny afternoon, with a glass of chicha morada, is one of the world's great meals. The Larco Museum's pre-Columbian gold collection is extraordinary.

  • Acclimatize in Cusco for 48 hours before any strenuous activity — altitude sickness is serious above 3,000m
  • Machu Picchu tickets must be booked months ahead online at machupicchu.gob.pe — they genuinely sell out
  • The Inca Trail (4 days) requires booking a full year ahead in high season through a licensed operator
  • Carry altitude sickness medication (acetazolamide/Diamox) — consult your doctor before travel
  • Try ceviche, lomo saltado, anticuchos, chupe de camarones — Peruvian food is exceptional
  • The scenic Vistadome train from Ollantaytambo to Aguas Calientes (the Machu Picchu train town) is spectacular
