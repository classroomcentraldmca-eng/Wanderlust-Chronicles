🇬🇷 Greece

Whitewashed Cliffs & Crystalline Seas

⭐ 4.8 Rating📅 May–June, September–October💰 $80–$200/day🛂 Schengen Visa

Greece is the birthplace of democracy, philosophy, mathematics, and Western civilization itself — and also one of the most strikingly beautiful countries on earth. The islands alone — more than 6,000 of them scattered across the Aegean and Ionian Seas — could fill a lifetime of travel. Add the mainland's extraordinary antiquity, Mediterranean cuisine built on olive oil and the sea, and the warmth of genuine Greek hospitality, and the picture becomes irresistible.

Athens: Ancient & Modern in Constant Dialogue

Athens is chaotic, loud, perpetually under construction, and inexplicably magnificent. The Acropolis rising above the city — the Parthenon, the Erechtheion, the Temple of Athena Nike — is one of the world's most emotionally powerful sights. Climb it at sunset and watch the golden light turn the Pentelic marble luminous. The National Archaeological Museum is the world's finest collection of ancient Greek art. Below the rock, the Plaka and Monastiraki neighborhoods offer mezze, ouzo, and extremely long conversations with strangers.

The Cyclades: Island Hopping Paradise

Santorini's caldera view from Oia is one of the world's most photographed images — and it earns every shot. But Santorini is also one of the most crowded destinations in Europe in summer. Visit in May or September, stay in Imerovigli rather than Oia, and explore the volcanic beaches and the excavated Bronze Age city of Akrotiri. For a more relaxed experience: Paros and Naxos offer comparable beauty with fewer tourists; Milos has some of the most dramatic coastline in the Aegean; Folegandros is almost untouched.

Meteora: Monasteries in the Sky

Meteora, in central Greece, is one of the world's most jaw-dropping landscapes: ancient Eastern Orthodox monasteries perched on impossibly vertical rock pinnacles, accessible only by staircases carved into the stone. Six of the original 24 monasteries survive and remain active religious sites. Visit at dawn or dusk when the light on the rocks turns golden and the tourist coaches have not yet arrived.

Crete: Greece's Largest & Most Diverse Island

Crete is a destination in itself — large enough to require a week minimum. The Palace of Knossos, Europe's oldest city, tells the story of the Minoan civilization 5,000 years old. The Samaria Gorge is one of Europe's longest hikes. The old city of Chania, with its Venetian harbor and crumbling Ottomanmosque, is among Greece's most beautiful towns. Cretan cuisine — dakos, sfakianes pites, lamb with stamnagathi — is extraordinary.

  • Visit Santorini and Mykonos in May or September — August is overwhelmingly crowded and expensive
  • Book island ferry tickets weeks ahead for summer; Aegean Speed Lines and SeaJets are reliable
  • Greek food is best at family-run tavernas away from tourist strips — look for locals eating there
  • Hire a car or scooter on larger islands — beaches that require 4km of walking are often empty
  • Many archaeological sites offer free entry on certain Sundays between November and March
